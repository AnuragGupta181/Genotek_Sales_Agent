1. What Problem Does This Agent Solve?
Nate's first question:
"What's the single workflow that, if automated, would change the trajectory of this business?"
Genotek Global sells expansion joint covers across India, GCC, and Southeast Asia. One person — AK (Anutashaya Kumar) — handles 86% of all email traffic. She processes 16 quotes per business day. She's fast (33-minute quote turnaround when pricing is available). She's reliable. And the business is hemorrhaging revenue anyway.
Why?
Three numbers from the 18-month email audit tell the story:
The Revenue Leak
pie title Where 3,565 Quote Threads End Up
    "Got Follow-Up (50.8%)" : 50.8
    "ZERO Follow-Up (39.1%)" : 39.1
    "Dormant (10.1%)" : 10.1
39.1% of all quotation threads receive zero follow-up.
That's 1,394 threads over 18 months. At a 12% conversion rate, that's roughly 167 orders that died by silence — not by rejection. Nobody said no. AK just didn't follow up, because she's buried under 146+ concurrent projects with no system telling her what to chase.
The second number:
7% project completion rate.
Of 211 tracked projects, only 15 reached completion. The rest stalled somewhere in the pipeline — pricing not returned, specs incomplete, follow-up never sent, PO stuck in approval.
The third number:
96 days.
That's the threshold after which a deal is effectively dead. No thread in the audit data recovered after 96 days of silence.
This is not an AK problem.
AK executes everything — 522 decisions per 18 months on her own authority. But she has ZERO pricing authority. Every single quote requires escalation to Bijoy, Shylesh, or Niranjan. The bottleneck is the system, not the person.
Nate's second question:
"What does the agent actually DO — and what does it absolutely NOT do?"
This agent is a
Sales Support Agent
— not an autonomous sales manager. It supports AK and the team. Specifically:
Agent DOES
Agent NEVER DOES
Classify incoming RFQs by region, product, urgency
Set any price, ever
Route pricing requests to correct authority
Approve discounts
Draft quotes using verified templates
Send to client without human approval (at Level 0)
Schedule and send project-specific follow-ups
Commit delivery dates without supplier confirmation
Flag deals going cold (approaching 96-day death)
Negotiate with clients
Track supplier status and lead times
Approve POs (Shylesh's job)
Store learnings in Open Brain memory
Make strategic decisions
